A 6 months long master plan for learning piano accordion

Goals:

  • Understand basics of music theory
  • Learn the basics of accordion playing
  • Practice simple to intermediate songs
  • Learn and practice the basics of playing by ear

1st Month: 18 Nov-15 Dec

Objective:

  • Getting familiar with the instrument
  • Enhancing control over bellow and bass coordination
  • Transition from imitation to independent playing

Resources:

  • YouTube tutorials focused on technique.
  • Musescore for free sheet music.

week 1: 18 Nov - 24 Nov

  • Learn and practice scales and arpeggios on both hands: C major, G major and A minor
  • Focus on smooth bellow changes while maintaining constant volume

week 2: 25 Nov - 1 Dec

  • Practice scales and arpeggios on both hands: C major, G major and A minor
  • Focus on smooth bellow changes while maintaining constant volume

week 3: 2 Dec - 8 Dec

  • Learn 1 song: Ode to joy
  • Focus on playing at a steady tempo

week 4: 9 Dec - 15 Dec

  • Learn 1 song: Ode to joy
  • Focus on playing at a steady tempo

2nd Month: 16 Dec - 12 Jan

Objective:

  • Play familiar songs without relying on tutorials or sheet music.
  • Improve ear-training skills.

Resources:

  • Ear training apps or websites
  • Online accordion forums for ear training entries

week 1: 16 Dec - 22 Dec

  • Practice identifying notes and intervals using ear-training apps.
  • Pick 1 song and try to figure it out by ear. Start with the melody, then add bass accompaniment.

week 2: 23 Dec - 29 Dec

  • Practice identifying notes and intervals using ear-training apps.
  • Pick 1 song and try to figure it out by ear. Start with the melody, then add bass accompaniment.

week 3: 30 Dec - 5 Jan

  • Learn to play 1 popular songs by ear (e.g., "Happy Birthday" or a simple folk tune).
  • Record and analyze. Identify and correct your mistakes

week 4: 6 Jan - 12 Jan

  • Learn to play 1 popular songs by ear (e.g., "Happy Birthday" or a simple folk tune).
  • Record and analyze. Identify and correct your mistakes

3rd Month: 13 Jan - 9 Feb

Objective:

  • Expand Repertoire and Introduce Expression
  • Add emotional depth to your playing with dynamics and articulation.
  • Learn songs from different genres.

Resources:

  • Online accordion sheet music websites.
  • Dynamics-focused tutorials on YouTube.

week 1: 13 Jan - 19 Jan

  • Choose 1 new piece (can be a waltz). Learn and play it
  • Focus on dynamics: crescendos, diminuendos, and staccato/legato transitions.

week 2: 20 Jan - 26 Jan

  • Choose 1 new piece (can be a polka). Learn and play it
  • Focus on dynamics: crescendos, diminuendos, and staccato/legato transitions.

week 3: 27 Jan - 2 Feb

  • Learn a slower, expressive piece (e.g., "La Vie en Rose").
  • Experiment with adding personal flair or improvisation.

week 4: 3 Feb - 9 Mar

  • Learn a slower, expressive piece (e.g., "La Vie en Rose").
  • Experiment with adding personal flair or improvisation.

4th Month: 10 Feb - 16 Feb

Objective:

  • Improve finger dexterity and bass accompaniment complexity.
  • Start improvising.

Resources:

  • Improvisation tutorials.
  • Backing tracks to practice rhythm and accompaniment.

week 1: 10 Feb - 16 Feb

  • Practice advanced scales (e.g., chromatic) and fast runs on the keyboard side.
  • Experiment with complex bass patterns (e.g., waltz or tango rhythms).

week 2: 17 Feb - 23 Feb

  • Practice advanced scales (e.g., chromatic) and fast runs on the keyboard side.
  • Experiment with complex bass patterns (e.g., waltz or tango rhythms).

week 3: 24 Feb - 2 Mar

  • Improvise over simple chord progressions using the C major and A minor scales.
  • Learn 1 challenging piece (e.g., "Libertango" by Astor Piazzolla).

week 4: 3 Mar - 9 Mar

  • Improvise over simple chord progressions using the C major and A minor scales.
  • Learn 1 challenging piece (e.g., "Libertango" by Astor Piazzolla).

5th Month: 10 Mar - 6 Apr

Objective:

  • Create a polished repertoire for casual performances.
  • Refine technical and expressive elements.

Resources:

  • Accordion performance videos for inspiration.
  • Online communities like r/accordion for feedback

week 1: 10 Mar - 16 Mar

  • Choose 4-5 favorite pieces from your repertoire.
  • Focus on perfecting transitions, tempo, and expression.

week 2: 17 Mar - 23 Mar

  • Choose 4-5 favorite pieces from your repertoire.
  • Focus on perfecting transitions, tempo, and expression.

week 3: 24 Mar - 30 Mar

  • Perform for friends, family, or record videos for feedback.
  • Adjust based on feedback and work on weaker areas.

week 4: 31 Mar - 6 Apr

  • Perform for friends, family, or record videos for feedback.
  • Adjust based on feedback and work on weaker areas.

6th Month: 7 Apr - 4 May

Objective:

  • Develop your unique style.
  • Start arranging or composing your own pieces.

Resources:

  • Online tools for composition (e.g., Musescore).
  • Videos on accordion arrangements.

week 1: 7 Apr - 13 Apr

  • Arrange a simple piece you love for the accordion.
  • Experiment with adding embellishments or variations to your playing.

week 2: 14 Apr - 20 Apr

  • Arrange a simple piece you love for the accordion.
  • Experiment with adding embellishments or variations to your playing.

week 3: 21 Apr - 27 Apr

  • Compose a short melody or simple song using your skills.
  • Plan for your next goals in accordion playing (e.g., mastering specific styles).

week 4: 28 Apr - 4 May

  • Compose a short melody or simple song using your skills.
  • Plan for your next goals in accordion playing (e.g., mastering specific styles).
